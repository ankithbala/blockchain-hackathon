Challenge 5

Real Time Record Maintenance

Use cases:Land Records Management Through Use of Blockchain


DESCRIPTION OF USE CASE

The Revenue department of Government of Karnataka maintains land records in digital form

The land records are important from a the perspective of a) title of a land unit needs to be confirmed with one owner and b) there needs to be a clear trail & record of all transactions done regarding the unit

The land record are used by various other departments/stakeholders concerning a particular land e.g. registrar office for sale/registration, banks/co -­‐ operatives for farm loans, land acquisition department for village/town extension, roads, culverts etc.

We need to have a trustworthy real -­‐ time record maintenance system capturing various attributes pertaining to a land

Know more

AS-­IS SCENARIO:

Record of rights, tenancy and crop (RTC) on a land in Karnataka is maintained on an online mutation application named BHOOMI Change to RTCs due to sale, partition, inheritance, pledge, release, government/court order etc. happen only on BHOOMI without anphysical record keeping/human intervention The mutation process is initiated at taluka level and then replicated at the state level to establish the record of rights (ROR) at a stalevel The state level information on land records is shared electronically with different stakeholders for various purposes –

Registration Department – The transaction to transfer the rights except inheritance happens in Sub Registrar Office. KAVERIsystem of the Stamps and Registration department – talks electronically to BHOOMI. This has not only helped prospective buyebut has also gone a long way in enhancing the credibility of the registration department

Banks and Co-­‐operative Institutions – Banks and co-­‐operative institutions are enabled to electronically raise digitally signed requefor pledge or release of any land. This has helped them to access the land records database for confirming ownership, extenowned by the farmer, other liabilities that farmer has and also monitor the status of requests raised by them

Land Acquiring Bodies – BHOOSWADEENA, an automated land acquisition system, talks electronically to BHOOMI, for variouactivities such as village/town extension, roads, culverts, reservoirs, canals, military camps, railways, industries etc., as needed bthe government

Survey Department – A pre-­‐mutation sketch is mandatory and no transaction in sub-­‐registrar offices can happen without the sketcMOJANI, a software for meant for this purpose, if fully integrated with BHOOMI to achieve this objective

Others – Crop details of ROR are used by department of Economics and Statistics, Agriculture and horticulture. Forest departmeuses ROR data for protecting forest land

TO-­BE SCENARIO

The information pertaining to a land record (ownership, extent of ownership, farm credit declaration, notification from land acquisition authorities, government/court orders etc.) shouldn’t be centralized, lying on a single/few servers under the ownership of only a few parties – BHOOMI, KAVERI, BHOOSWADEENA, MOJANI etc.

The information should be readily available to all the interested parties (individuals, banks, registration department, survey department etc.), with no dependency on a particular stakeholder/owner or seeking information from one entity, with relevant rights to read/write the right set of information

The authencity of the information in such a decentralized scenario is of utmost importance – in spite of the information lying in a decentralized manner, nobody should be able to modify/edit the information entered previously Any piece of information appended to the existing set should be agreeable to all the relevant stakeholders The transactions should happen on a real-­‐time basis, to avoid any situation of two transactions of opposite nature putting forth their claim on a single asset The transactions should be traceable and transparent – any changes made to land records should be traceable with the set of information attached at each step
